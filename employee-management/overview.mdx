Frappe HR models every stage of the employee lifecycle as a set of linked doctypes. Whether you are bringing on a new hire, moving someone to a different department, or processing a final settlement, each action is tracked, auditable, and reversible.

## Lifecycle stages

The employee lifecycle in Frappe HR moves through four broad stages:

1. **Onboarding** — A new hire completes structured activities before their employee record is created.
2. **Active employment** — The employee profile holds personal details, employment terms, leave settings, and salary information.
3. **Transfers and promotions** — Changes to department, designation, grade, or company are recorded with full property history.
4. **Separation** — An offboarding checklist, exit interview, and Full & Final Statement conclude the employment relationship.

## Key doctypes

| Doctype | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `Employee` | Central record for all employee data |
| `Employee Onboarding` | Tracks pre-joining activities and creates the Employee record |
| `Employee Onboarding Template` | Reusable activity checklist for onboarding |
| `Employee Transfer` | Records a change of department, designation, branch, or company |
| `Employee Promotion` | Records a change in grade or salary with an effective date |
| `Employee Property History` | Child table storing before/after snapshots for transfers and promotions |
| `Employee Separation` | Tracks offboarding activities via an activity checklist |
| `Employee Separation Template` | Reusable activity checklist for offboarding |
| `Exit Interview` | Captures the exit discussion outcome and questionnaire |
| `Full and Final Statement` | Settles outstanding payables, receivables, and assets on exit |

## How the lifecycle connects

The doctypes are linked so that actions flow naturally from one stage to the next:

- A submitted **Job Offer** can be used to create an **Employee Onboarding** document.
- When all mandatory onboarding activities are complete, the onboarding document creates an **Employee** record.
- During active employment, **Employee Transfer** and **Employee Promotion** documents update the Employee record in place and write rows to the internal work history.
- When the employee leaves, an **Employee Separation** document coordinates offboarding tasks, an **Exit Interview** captures the conversation, and a **Full and Final Statement** settles dues.

<Info>
All boarding documents (onboarding and separation) automatically create a **Project** with individual **Tasks** for each activity. This means you can track progress through the standard Frappe project interface and assign tasks to specific users or roles.
</Info>
