---
title: "Expense claims overview"
description: "Manage employee expense reimbursements, advances, and travel requests with integrated accounting in ERPNext."
---

Frappe HR provides a complete expense management workflow covering everything from raising a travel request before a trip to reconciling advances and submitting final expense claims for reimbursement. All transactions post directly to ERPNext's accounting ledgers.

## How the workflow fits together

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request (optional)">
    Employees submit a **Travel Request** before travelling. The request captures the itinerary, purpose, travel type (Domestic/International), estimated costs, and funding requirements. Once approved, it authorises the trip.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Advance (optional)">
    If the employee needs funds upfront, they raise an **Employee Advance**. The advance is approved and disbursed via a Bank or Journal Entry, posting a debit to the configured Employee Advance (Receivable) account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Claim">
    After incurring expenses, the employee creates an **Expense Claim** listing each expense item with its type, claimed amount, and receipt attachment. They select an expense approver and submit the document.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approval">
    The expense approver reviews each line, sets a sanctioned amount (which may differ from the claimed amount), and sets the Approval Status to **Approved** or **Rejected**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Payment">
    Once approved, the payable amount (grand total minus any advance already allocated) is reimbursed to the employee via a **Payment Entry** or **Journal Entry** depending on your Accounts Settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## ERPNext accounting integration

Expense Claims extend ERPNext's `AccountsController`, so every approval and payment creates proper General Ledger entries:

| Event | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Claim approved | Expense account (per Expense Claim Type) | Employee Payable account |
| Advance allocated | Employee Advance (Receivable) account | Employee Payable account |
| Tax/charge added | Tax account head | Employee Payable account |
| Payment made | Employee Payable account | Bank / Cash account |

<Note>
If **Make Payment via Journal Entry** is enabled in Accounts Settings, payments are created as Journal Entries instead of Payment Entries.
</Note>
