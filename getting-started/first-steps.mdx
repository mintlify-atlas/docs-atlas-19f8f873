title: "First steps"
description: "Configure Frappe HR after installation: set up your company, add employees, and prepare the system for leave and payroll management."
---

After installing Frappe HR, there are a few configuration steps to complete before you can use leave management, payroll, and attendance features. This guide walks you through the minimum setup required to get your HR system running.

<Info>
  Frappe HR requires at least one **Company** record in ERPNext before you can create employees or run payroll. If you are starting from a fresh installation, creating a company is the first thing to do.
</Info>

## Setup checklist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in as Administrator">
    Open your Frappe HR site and log in with your administrator credentials.

    - For a local bench install: `http://hrms.localhost:8080`
    - For Docker: `http://localhost:8000`
    - Username: **Administrator**

    After logging in, open the **Frappe HR** app from the home screen. This takes you to the HR workspace at `/desk/people`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set up a company">
    Frappe HR is tightly integrated with ERPNext. All employees, salary structures, and payroll entries belong to a company. You must create at least one company before adding employees.

    1. In the search bar, type **Company** and open the doctype.
    2. Click **New**.
    3. Fill in the **Company Name**, **Default Currency**, and **Country**.
    4. Save the record.

    <Tip>
      The country you select determines which regional payroll features are available. For example, selecting **India** unlocks HRA exemption calculations and professional tax configurations.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure HR settings">
    HR Settings is the central configuration doctype for system-wide HR behaviour. Review it before creating employees.

    1. Search for **HR Settings** in the search bar and open it.
    2. Review the following key settings:

    | Setting | Description |
    |---------|-------------|
    | **Leave Approver Mandatory** | Requires every leave application to have an approver assigned |
    | **Expense Approver Mandatory** | Requires expense claims to have an approver |
    | **Max Working Hours Against Timesheet** | Cap on hours per timesheet entry |
    | **Auto Leave Encashment** | Automatically creates leave encashment entries at period end |
    | **Retirement Age** | Used to flag employees approaching retirement |

    3. Save your changes.

    <Note>
      You can return to HR Settings at any time to adjust these values as your processes evolve.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your first employee">
    With a company in place, you can now create an employee record.

    1. Navigate to **HR > Employee > New Employee** or search for **Employee** and click **New**.
    2. Fill in the required fields:
       - **First Name** and **Last Name**
       - **Company** — select the company you created
       - **Date of Joining**
       - **Employment Type** (Full-time, Part-time, Contract, etc.)
       - **Department** and **Designation** — create these on the fly if they don't exist yet
    3. In the **Job Profile** section, set the **Reports To** field if you want to configure an approval chain.
    4. Save the employee.

    <Tip>
      Add the employee's work email in the **Contact Details** tab. This is used for leave notifications, interview reminders, and daily work summaries.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set up a holiday list">
    A Holiday List defines the public holidays and weekly off days for your organisation. It is required to correctly calculate leave balances and attendance.

    1. Search for **Holiday List** and click **New**.
    2. Enter a **Holiday List Name** (for example, "India 2025" or "US Holidays 2025").
    3. Set the **From Date** and **To Date** for the year.
    4. Click **Add Weekly Holidays** to automatically mark your weekly off days (for example, Saturday and Sunday).
    5. Add individual public holidays in the **Holidays** table.
    6. Save the holiday list.

    To assign this list to your company, open the **Company** record and set the **Default Holiday List** field.

    <Note>
      You can also assign different holiday lists to individual employees in the **Attendance and Leave Details** section of the Employee record. Employee-level holiday lists take priority over the company default.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a leave policy and assign it">
    A Leave Policy defines how many days of each leave type employees are entitled to per year. Assigning a policy to an employee allocates those leave days to their account.

    **Create leave types**

    1. Search for **Leave Type** and create the types you need (for example, Casual Leave, Sick Leave, Earned Leave).
    2. Configure each type's settings: whether it carries forward, whether it is encashable, and whether it requires allocation.

    **Create a leave policy**

    1. Search for **Leave Policy** and click **New**.
    2. Give it a descriptive name (for example, "Standard Leave Policy 2025").
    3. In the **Leave Policy Details** table, add each leave type and the number of days allocated per year.
    4. Save the policy.

    **Assign the policy to an employee**

    1. Search for **Leave Policy Assignment** and click **New**.
    2. Select the **Employee** and the **Leave Policy**.
    3. Set the **Effective From** date and the **Leave Period** (or enter a custom date range).
    4. Click **Grant Leave** to create the leave allocations for that employee.

    <Tip>
      Use **Leave Period** to bulk-assign policies to all employees at once via the **Grant Leaves** button on the Leave Period doctype. This is faster than creating individual assignments.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>
