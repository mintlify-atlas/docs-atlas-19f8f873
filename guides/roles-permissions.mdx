Frappe HR uses the Frappe Framework role-based permission system. Each user is assigned one or more roles, and each role grants read, write, create, submit, cancel, and amend permissions on specific doctypes.

## Built-in roles

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="HR Manager" icon="user-tie">
    Full access to all HR and payroll doctypes. Can modify HR Settings and Payroll Settings. Assigned to HR administrators responsible for running payroll, managing employees, and approving escalations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="HR User" icon="user">
    Read and limited write access to HR doctypes. Can view HR Settings but cannot modify them. Suitable for HR staff who process day-to-day HR operations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Leave Approver" icon="calendar-check">
    Permission to approve or reject Leave Application documents. Does not grant general HR access. Assigned to managers and team leads.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Expense Approver" icon="receipt">
    Permission to approve or reject Expense Claim documents. Does not grant general HR access. Assigned to managers and finance team members.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Employee Self Service" icon="id-card">
    Allows employees to submit leave applications, expense claims, and view their own salary slips. The standard role for all regular employees in the system.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Employee" icon="person">
    Base role for all employees. Grants read-only access to reference data such as holiday lists and HR Settings. Typically combined with Employee Self Service.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Assigning roles to users

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the user record">
    Go to **Settings → User** and open the user you want to configure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to the Roles table">
    Scroll down to the **Roles** section on the user form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add roles">
    Click **Add Row** and select the role to assign. Repeat for each required role.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. Role changes take effect on the user's next login or page refresh.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Avoid assigning the **System Manager** role to regular HR staff. System Manager grants unrestricted access to all doctypes and settings. Use **HR Manager** instead.
</Tip>

## Department-level leave approver

Frappe HR supports assigning a default leave approver at the department level so that employees do not need to select an approver each time they submit a leave application.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the department record">
    Go to **HR → Departments** and open the relevant department.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the leave approver">
    In the **Leave Approver** field, select the user who should approve leave applications for this department.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. When an employee in this department submits a leave application, the approver field is pre-filled automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
If **Leave Approver Mandatory In Leave Application** is enabled in HR Settings, employees must have a valid approver assigned either at the department level or selected manually before they can submit.
</Note>

## Department-level expense approver

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the department record">
    Go to **HR → Departments** and open the relevant department.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the expense approver">
    In the **Expense Approver** field, select the user who should approve expense claims for this department.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. When an employee in this department creates an expense claim, the approver field is pre-filled.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Preventing self-approval

To prevent managers from approving their own requests even when they hold the Leave Approver or Expense Approver role:

- Enable **Prevent self approval for leaves even if user has permissions** in HR Settings → Leaves tab.
- Enable **Prevent self approval for expense claims even if user has permissions** in HR Settings → Expenses tab.

With these settings enabled, the system blocks approval by the same user who submitted the document, regardless of their role.

## Multi-level approval workflows

For organizations that require more than one level of approval, Frappe Framework's built-in **Workflow** engine can be applied to Leave Application and Expense Claim doctypes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a workflow">
    Go to **Settings → Workflow** and create a new workflow for the **Leave Application** doctype.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define states">
    Add states such as `Draft`, `Pending Manager Approval`, `Pending HR Approval`, and `Approved`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define transitions">
    Create transition rules that specify which role can move the document from one state to the next.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activate the workflow">
    Enable the workflow. The standard submit/approve buttons are replaced by your custom workflow action buttons.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Activating a workflow on Leave Application or Expense Claim overrides the default single-approver flow. Test workflows in a staging environment before deploying to production.
</Warning>
