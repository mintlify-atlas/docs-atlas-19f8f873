---
title: "Leave policies"
description: "Group leave entitlements into reusable policies, assign them to employees, and track balances through the leave ledger."
---

Frappe HR uses three linked documents to move from a company-wide entitlement policy down to an individual employee balance:

1. **Leave Policy** — defines what types of leave and how many days employees are entitled to.
2. **Leave Policy Assignment** — links a policy to one or more employees for a specific Leave Period.
3. **Leave Allocation** — the actual balance record per employee, per leave type, per period. Created automatically from an assignment.

## Leave period

Before creating a policy assignment you need a Leave Period. A Leave Period is a named date range (e.g. `Jan 2025 – Dec 2025`) scoped to a company.

Go to **HR > Leave Period > New** and fill in:

- **Leave Period Name** — descriptive label.
- **Company** — the company this period applies to.
- **From Date / To Date** — start and end of the entitlement year.
- **Is Active** — only active periods appear in assignment and application forms.

<Info>
You can have multiple active leave periods for different companies in a multi-company setup.
</Info>

## Leave policy

A Leave Policy is a submittable document that lists leave types with their annual entitlement quantities.

Go to **HR > Leave Policy > New**.

- **Title** — a descriptive name such as `Standard Employee Policy 2025`.
- **Leave Policy Details** (table) — add one row per leave type:
  - **Leave Type** — link to a Leave Type record.
  - **Annual Allocation** — number of days per year.

Submit the policy before assigning it. Submitted policies carry naming format `HR-LPOL-YYYY-#####`.

<Note>
Leave Policies are submittable and can be amended. Amending a submitted policy creates a new version while preserving the original for audit purposes.
</Note>

## Leave policy assignment

A Leave Policy Assignment links a submitted Leave Policy to employees for a specific Leave Period.

Go to **HR > Leave Policy Assignment > New**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the assignment basis">
    Choose whether to assign to an individual **Employee**, a **Department**, a **Designation**, or the entire **Company**. Bulk assignment modes generate individual assignment records for each matching employee.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the leave period">
    Select the active Leave Period for which entitlements should apply.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the leave policy">
    Link the submitted Leave Policy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the effective date">
    The **Effective From** date controls pro-rated allocation for employees who join mid-period.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the assignment">
    On submit, Frappe HR creates Leave Allocation records for each employee and each leave type in the policy. The allocation quantity is automatically pro-rated if the employee's Date of Joining falls after the leave period start date.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Bulk assignment

To assign a policy to multiple employees at once:

1. Open **HR > Leave Policy Assignment**.
2. Set **Assign Based On** to Department, Designation, or Company.
3. Fill the target value (e.g. select the department name).
4. Submit — one assignment record is created per matching employee.

<Tip>
Use bulk assignment at the start of each year to renew allocations for all employees in one action rather than creating individual assignments.
</Tip>

## Leave allocation

Leave Allocations are created automatically by the Leave Policy Assignment, but you can also create them manually at **HR > Leave Allocation > New**.

Key fields on a Leave Allocation:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Employee** | The employee whose balance this record represents. |
| **Leave Type** | The category of leave. |
| **Leave Period** | The active period this allocation belongs to. |
| **New Leaves Allocated** | Days freshly granted for this period. |
| **Carry Forwarded Leaves** | Balance rolled in from the previous period (populated automatically if the leave type allows carry-forward). |
| **Total Leaves Allocated** | Sum of new and carry-forwarded leaves. |
| **From Date / To Date** | Validity window — must fall within the leave period. |

Submit the allocation to make the balance available to the employee.

## Leave balance and the leave ledger

Every leave transaction — allocation, application, encashment, or expiry — creates a **Leave Ledger Entry**. The current leave balance is the sum of all ledger entries for an employee and leave type within the active period.

You can inspect entries at **HR > Leave Ledger Entry**. Useful ledger entry types:

| Transaction Type | Effect on Balance |
|---|---|
| Leave Allocation | Adds days |
| Leave Application | Deducts days |
| Leave Encashment | Deducts days |
| Carry Forward Expiry | Deducts carry-forwarded days after the expiry window |

<Note>
Do not delete Leave Ledger Entries directly. Cancel the source document (allocation, application, or encashment) to reverse the ledger impact.
</Note>
