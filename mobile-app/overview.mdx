Frappe HR includes a mobile app built with Vue and Ionic Framework. It is a Progressive Web App (PWA) served directly from your Frappe HR instance — no separate installation or app store is required.

## Accessing the app

Open your Frappe HR instance URL in a mobile browser and navigate to `/hrms`. You will be prompted to log in if you are not already authenticated.

<Note>
The mobile app is available to any user with an Employee record linked to their account. Users without a linked Employee record will see an error screen.
</Note>

## Installing as a PWA

Installing the app to your home screen gives you a native-like experience with a dedicated icon and full-screen display.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open in your mobile browser">
    Navigate to `https://your-instance.com/hrms` in Chrome (Android) or Safari (iOS).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open browser menu">
    On Android, tap the three-dot menu and select **Add to Home screen**. On iOS, tap the Share icon and select **Add to Home Screen**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm installation">
    Confirm the name and tap **Add**. The Frappe HR icon will appear on your home screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch and log in">
    Tap the icon to open the app. Log in with your Frappe HR credentials.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Home screen

After logging in, the home screen shows:

- A **Check In / Check Out** panel (if your administrator has enabled mobile check-in)
- Quick links to common actions: Request Attendance, Request a Shift, Request Leave, Claim an Expense, Request an Advance, and View Salary Slips
- A panel showing pending requests that need your attention

## Administrator settings

Two settings in **HR Settings** control mobile app behaviour:

| Setting | Description |
|---|---|
| Allow Employee Checkin from Mobile App | Shows the check-in/check-out panel on the home screen |
| Allow Geolocation Tracking | Captures latitude and longitude coordinates when an employee checks in or out |

## Technology

The mobile app is built with [Vue 3](https://vuejs.org), [Ionic Framework](https://ionicframework.com), and [Frappe UI](https://frappeui.com). It uses `vite-plugin-pwa` and Workbox for service worker and offline caching support. Firebase is used for push notifications.
