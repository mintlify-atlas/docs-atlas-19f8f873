title: "Gratuity"
description: "Configure gratuity rules, calculate gratuity for departing employees, and integrate with Full and Final settlement."

Gratuity is a lump-sum payment made to an employee upon separation after completing a minimum number of years in service. Frappe HR calculates gratuity automatically based on configurable slab rules and the employee's last salary.

## Gratuity rule

A **Gratuity Rule** defines how gratuity is calculated: which salary components are included in the base, the minimum service years required, and the slab-based multipliers.

Go to **Payroll → Gratuity Rule → New**.

### Rule fields

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Calculate Gratuity Amount Based On** | `Current Slab` — applies the fraction for the slab the employee falls in. `Sum of all previous slabs` — adds up fractions for each completed slab progressively. |
| **Work Experience Calculation Method** | `Round off Work Experience` — rounds total years to the nearest integer. `Take Exact Completed Years` — uses the floor of the total years. `Manual` — allows manual entry of work experience on the gratuity record. |
| **Total Working Days Per Year** | Number of working days per year used to convert total days to years (default: 365). |
| **Minimum Year for Gratuity** | Employees must complete at least this many years to be eligible. |
| **Applicable Earnings Component** | The salary components whose amounts form the gratuity base (e.g., Basic Salary). These must be part of the employee's salary structure. |
| **Gratuity Rule Slabs** | A table of slabs: **From Year**, **To Year**, and **Fraction of Applicable Earnings**. |

### Example: Indian gratuity rule

Under the Indian Payment of Gratuity Act, gratuity is 15 days of last drawn wages per year of service (minimum 5 years).

| From Year | To Year | Fraction of Applicable Earnings |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 0 | 0.0577 |

- Calculation method: **Sum of all previous slabs**
- Minimum years: **5**
- Set **From Year** and **To Year** to `0` to apply a single fraction for all years of service.

```python
# Gratuity = (last basic salary / 26) * 15 * years_of_service
# Fraction = 15/26 ≈ 0.5769 per year, but calculated as:
# fraction_of_applicable_earnings = 15 / 26 ≈ 0.5769
# The system computes: basic_salary * years * fraction
```

<Note>
The fraction `15/26` approximates 15 days out of 26 working days per month. Configure this as `0.5769` in the **Fraction of Applicable Earnings** column.
</Note>

## Calculating gratuity for a departing employee

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set the relieving date">
    Ensure the employee record has a **Relieving Date** set. This is required to calculate total working days. Go to the Employee form and update the relieving date under the **Exit** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a Gratuity record">
    Go to **Payroll → Gratuity → New**. Select the employee and the gratuity rule.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review calculated values">
    On save, the system calculates:

    - **Current Work Experience** — derived from date of joining and relieving date, adjusted for LWP or absent days as configured in Payroll Settings.
    - **Amount** — computed from the last submitted salary slip, the applicable earnings components, and the slab rules.

    Review both values before submitting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose payment method">
    - **Pay via Salary Slip** — enables the **Pay via Salary Slip** toggle. Set the **Payroll Date** and the **Salary Component** to use. Submitting creates an Additional Salary record that is picked up in the next payroll run.
    - **Direct payment** — leave **Pay via Salary Slip** disabled. Set the **Expense Account** and **Payable Account**. Submitting creates GL entries directly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the gratuity record">
    Submit the record. Depending on the payment method:

    - If paying via salary slip: an Additional Salary is created with `ref_doctype = Gratuity`.
    - If paying directly: debit/credit GL entries are posted (Gratuity Expense debit, Gratuity Payable credit).

    The status moves to **Unpaid** until the full amount is settled.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Work experience calculation

The system computes total working days as:

```python
total_working_days = (relieving_date - date_of_joining).days

# Adjust for LWP (if Payroll Based On = Leave)
total_working_days -= total_lwp_days

# Or adjust for absences (if Payroll Based On = Attendance)
total_working_days -= total_absent_days

work_experience = total_working_days / total_working_days_per_year
```

LWP days are counted only for leave types with **Is LWP** (Leave Without Pay) enabled.

## Gratuity amount calculation

The base amount is fetched from the employee's **last submitted salary slip**. The system looks up the amounts of the components listed in **Applicable Earnings Component**.

### Current slab method

Applies the fraction for the single slab that the employee's work experience falls within:

```python
gratuity_amount = base_component_amount * experience * slab.fraction
```

### Sum of all previous slabs method

Adds up the fraction for each completed slab, then applies the remaining years to the final slab:

```python
for slab in slabs:
    if experience > slab.to_year:
        gratuity_amount += (slab.to_year - slab.from_year) * base * slab.fraction
    elif experience within slab:
        gratuity_amount += remaining_years * base * slab.fraction
        break
```

## Accounting entries

When a gratuity record is submitted with direct payment (not via salary slip), two GL entries are created:

| Account | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Gratuity Expense Account | Gratuity amount | — |
| Gratuity Payable Account | — | Gratuity amount |

The payable entry is linked to the employee as the party, so you can track outstanding gratuity obligations per employee.

Cancelling a submitted gratuity reverses both entries.

## Integration with Full and Final settlement

Gratuity is typically processed as part of the Full and Final (F&F) settlement when an employee exits. The workflow is:

1. Set the employee's **Relieving Date** on the Employee master.
2. Create and submit the **Gratuity** record.
3. If paying via salary slip, create the final salary slip (which will include the gratuity Additional Salary).
4. Process any outstanding leave encashment, expense claims, and loan settlements.
5. Record the final payment to clear the gratuity payable account.

<Info>
Frappe HR does not yet have a single "Full and Final Statement" doctype. Each component (gratuity, leave encashment, final salary slip) is processed through its respective doctype and linked through the employee record.
</Info>
