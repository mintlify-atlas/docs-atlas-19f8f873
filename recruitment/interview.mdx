title: "Interviews"
description: "Configure interview rounds, schedule sessions, collect structured feedback, and move applicants through the hiring pipeline."
---

Frappe HR structures interviews around pre-configured **Interview Rounds**. Each round defines the panel of interviewers, the skills to assess, and the minimum rating required to pass. Individual **Interview** documents are then scheduled against rounds for specific applicants.

## Interview Round configuration

Create an Interview Round in **HR > Recruitment > Interview Round** before scheduling any interviews.

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Round Name** | Unique name for the round (e.g., "Technical Round 1", "HR Interview") |
| **Interview Type** | Links to the Interview Type master for categorisation |
| **Designation** | If set, the round is only applicable to applicants for this designation |
| **Interviewers** | List of users who will conduct this round (must have the Interviewer role) |
| **Expected Average Rating** | Minimum average rating an applicant must achieve to clear this round |
| **Expected Skill Set** | Table of skills to be assessed; each interviewer rates these skills individually |

<Tip>
Assign the **Interviewer** role to all users who need to submit Interview Feedback. Users without this role cannot be selected in the Interviewers table.
</Tip>

## Scheduling an interview

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Job Applicant">
    Navigate to the Job Applicant record you want to interview.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create an interview">
    Click **Create Interview**. A dialog appears asking you to select an **Interview Round**.

    If the round has a designated designation set, it must match the applicant's designation. The system raises an error otherwise.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the schedule">
    The new Interview document is pre-filled with:
    - Job Applicant
    - Interview Round
    - Designation
    - Resume Link
    - Job Opening
    - Interviewers from the round's panel

    Set the **Scheduled On** date, **From Time**, and **To Time**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and notify">
    Save the Interview. All interviewers and the applicant are added as recipients for reminder emails.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Rescheduling

To change the time of a scheduled interview, use the **Reschedule Interview** action on the Interview document. Provide the new date and times. The system sends a rescheduling notification email to all interviewers and the applicant automatically.

<Note>
Each applicant can appear in a given Interview Round only once. Attempting to create a second submitted Interview for the same applicant and round raises a `DuplicateInterviewRoundError`.
</Note>

## Interview statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | Scheduled but not yet held |
| **Under Review** | Interview completed; awaiting feedback submission |
| **Cleared** | All feedback submitted; applicant passed the round |
| **Rejected** | Applicant did not meet the required rating |
| **Cancelled** | Interview was discarded |

Only Interviews with a **Cleared** or **Rejected** status can be submitted.

## Interview Feedback submission

After the interview session, each panellist submits their own **Interview Feedback** document.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Interview">
    The interviewer opens the Interview document (or uses the link in the reminder email).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit feedback">
    Click the **Submit Feedback** action. A form appears with:
    - **Skill Assessment** — rate each skill from the round's Expected Skill Set
    - **Feedback** — free-text qualitative comments
    - **Result** — select `Cleared` or `Rejected`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Feedback is validated and saved">
    The system validates that:
    - The submitter is in the interview's panel
    - The interview date has passed (feedback cannot be submitted before the scheduled date)
    - No duplicate feedback exists from the same interviewer

    The average rating across all skill assessments is calculated and stored.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Interview average is updated">
    On submission, the Interview document's **Average Rating** field is recalculated as the mean of all submitted feedback ratings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Only the user listed as an interviewer can submit feedback for that interview. Submitting on behalf of another interviewer is not permitted.
</Warning>

## Interview reminders

Frappe HR sends two types of automated reminder emails, both configured in **HR Settings**.

### Pre-interview reminder

The `send_interview_reminder` function runs on the **all** (every-minute) scheduler. It:

1. Checks if `send_interview_reminder` is enabled in HR Settings
2. Reads the `remind_before` interval (default: 1 hour)
3. Finds all Pending, non-reminded interviews scheduled within the next `remind_before` window
4. Sends an email to all interviewers and the applicant using the configured `interview_reminder_template`
5. Marks the interview as `reminded = 1` to avoid duplicate sends

To enable: go to **HR Settings > Hiring**, tick **Send Interview Reminder**, and select an **Interview Reminder Template**.

### Daily feedback reminder

The `send_daily_feedback_reminder` function runs on the **daily** scheduler. It:

1. Checks if `send_interview_feedback_reminder` is enabled in HR Settings
2. Finds all Interviews with status **Under Review** whose scheduled time has already passed
3. Identifies interviewers who have **not yet submitted** their feedback
4. Sends a reminder email to those interviewers using the `feedback_reminder_notification_template`

To enable: go to **HR Settings > Hiring**, tick **Send Interview Feedback Reminder**, and select a **Feedback Reminder Notification Template**.

## Moving an applicant to the next stage

When an Interview is submitted with a **Cleared** or **Rejected** status, the system displays a dialog asking whether to update the Job Applicant's status to match.

- Clicking **Mark as Accepted** sets the Job Applicant to `Accepted`
- Clicking **Mark as Rejected** sets the Job Applicant to `Rejected`

For multi-round hiring processes, you can schedule additional interviews in subsequent rounds without changing the applicant status, keeping the record in `Open` or `Replied` until all rounds are complete.

## Calendar view

Interviews support a **Calendar** view. Each interview is displayed in the colour associated with its status:

| Status | Colour |
|---|---|
| Pending | Light orange |
| Under Review | Light blue |
| Cleared | Light green |
| Rejected | Light red |
