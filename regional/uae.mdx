Frappe HR supports UAE-specific payroll compliance through pre-configured End-of-Service Gratuity (EOSG) rules. These rules are installed automatically when you run the setup for a UAE company.

## Setup

The UAE regional setup creates three Gratuity Rule documents based on UAE Labour Law. To run setup manually:

```bash
bench --site your-site.com execute hrms.regional.united_arab_emirates.setup.setup
```

## End-of-Service Gratuity

Under UAE Labour Law, employees who complete at least one year of continuous service are entitled to an end-of-service gratuity payment. The amount depends on the contract type, the reason for leaving (termination or resignation), and the number of completed years of service.

Frappe HR calculates gratuity using the **Gratuity** doctype. Assign one of the pre-configured rules to the Gratuity document for the employee.

### Gratuity rules

Three rules are installed for UAE:

#### Rule Under Limited Contract (UAE)

Applies when the employee is on a fixed-term (limited) contract, regardless of whether the separation is termination or resignation.

- **Calculation basis**: Sum of all previous slabs
- **Work experience method**: Take exact completed years
- **Minimum years**: 1

| Completed years | Daily wage fraction |
|---|---|
| Less than 1 year | 0 (no gratuity) |
| 1 to 5 years | 21/30 of daily wage per year |
| More than 5 years | 1 full daily wage per year |

#### Rule Under Unlimited Contract on termination (UAE)

Applies when an employee on an open-ended (unlimited) contract is terminated by the employer.

- **Calculation basis**: Current slab
- **Work experience method**: Take exact completed years
- **Minimum years**: 1

| Completed years | Daily wage fraction |
|---|---|
| Less than 1 year | 0 (no gratuity) |
| 1 to 5 years | 21/30 of daily wage per year |
| More than 5 years | 1 full daily wage per year |

#### Rule Under Unlimited Contract on resignation (UAE)

Applies when an employee on an unlimited contract voluntarily resigns. Gratuity is reduced for shorter service periods.

- **Calculation basis**: Current slab
- **Work experience method**: Take exact completed years
- **Minimum years**: 1

| Completed years | Daily wage fraction |
|---|---|
| Less than 1 year | 0 (no gratuity) |
| 1 to 3 years | (1/3) × (21/30) of daily wage per year |
| 3 to 5 years | (2/3) × (21/30) of daily wage per year |
| More than 5 years | 21/30 of daily wage per year |

<Info>
The fractions `21/30` and `1` reflect UAE Labour Law Article 132, which grants 21 calendar days' basic wage per year for the first five years and 30 days' wage per year thereafter.
</Info>

## Processing gratuity

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a Gratuity document">
    Go to **Payroll > Gratuity > New**. Select the employee and their last working date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the applicable rule">
    Choose the correct UAE gratuity rule based on the employee's contract type and reason for separation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify calculated amount">
    The system calculates the gratuity amount based on the applicable earnings and the slab fractions in the selected rule. Review the computed figure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit and process payment">
    Submit the Gratuity document and process the payment through the payroll or accounts module.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Verify that the employee's contract type and separation reason are correctly identified before selecting a rule. Using the wrong rule will result in an incorrect gratuity amount.
</Warning>

## Applicable earnings

UAE Labour Law calculates gratuity on the employee's **basic salary** (excluding allowances). In Frappe HR, set up the applicable earnings component in the **Gratuity Rule** or in the salary structure to ensure the correct base is used for calculation.

## Regional scope

Unlike India, the UAE regional module does not currently register `regional_overrides` in `hooks.py`. The UAE compliance is delivered entirely through the pre-configured Gratuity Rule documents created during setup. No custom functions override the standard payroll calculation path.
